# Asset attribution

## head.glb — "Lee Perry-Smith" head

`head.glb` is the **Lee Perry-Smith** head scan, by **Lee Perry-Smith /
Infinite-Realities** (https://ir-ltd.net/). It is long distributed with three.js
(`examples/models/gltf/LeePerrySmith/LeePerrySmith.glb`) and is licensed
**Creative Commons Attribution (CC-BY)**.

Used here as the sculpted base for the procedural `face` (the `headMesh` option):
the engine seats the procedural eyes in its sockets and a fedora on the crown.

> Head model by Lee Perry-Smith (Infinite-Realities), licensed CC-BY.

## CesiumMan.glb

`CesiumMan.glb` is the Cesium Man sample model from the glTF-Sample-Models
repository (Cesium / Khronos), used as the animated dancer.

## boat.glb — "Boat" by Pixel (Poly Pizza)

`boat.glb` is the **"Boat"** low-poly model by **Pixel**, from **Poly Pizza**
(https://poly.pizza/m/YwdXrwbN3o), licensed **Creative Commons Attribution
(CC-BY)**. Used as the static prop in the `water` example scene (a boat at the
origin); loaded through the engine's skin-less static-glTF path.

> Boat by Pixel (https://poly.pizza/m/YwdXrwbN3o), licensed CC-BY.

## rpm-half-body.glb

`rpm-half-body.glb` is a **Ready Player Me** half-body avatar, taken from RPM's
MIT-licensed demo repo [`readyplayerme/visage`](https://github.com/readyplayerme/visage)
(`public/half-body.glb`). It carries `LeftEye`/`RightEye` bones, ARKit + Oculus-viseme
blendshapes, and PBR textures — kept here as the reference **standard character**
(see `docs/rpm-character-reference.md`). Demo/development use; shipping RPM avatars
is governed by Ready Player Me's developer terms.
